Konkani Origin and Geography

Konkani communities are dominantly Catholic and Hindu in terms of religion. Some communities migrated to the state of Karnataka when Portuguese settlers began persecution during the 16th century. A few communities migrated further south to Kerala, highlighted in green on the map. Thus, due to geographical distance, there exists many Konkani dialects.

Konkani Customs

To the left, we see a traditional plate of Konkani vegetarian food. It is typically eaten on a banana leaf with multiple side dishes on special occasions like marriages, funerals and coming-of-age ceremonies. Some dishes include batata song, rice, daalithoy, paapad, bhaji and pickle.

On the right, you see bundles of flowers and a special type of grass which together forms a "choodi." Every Sunday of the month Sraavan, which coincides with the Indian monsoon season, married Konkani women make and offer choodi to other Konkani women. It is made with native flowers and herbaceous plants that have medicinal benefits.
