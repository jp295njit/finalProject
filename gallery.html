Gaud Saraswat Brahmins

Gaud Saraswat Brahmins are a small Hindu community in India that mostly speak Konkani, though many dialects exist. They migrated to Goa and the general Konkan region from the Saraswati river basin. After the invasion of Malik Kafur and persecution by the Portuguese, many Konkans left Goa and migrated south to regions in the states of Karnataka and Kerala. Most South Canarese Konkani groups follow Madhvacharya, which means the diet is primarily vegetarian and Hindu. The South Canarese dialect is influenced by the Kannada language while North Canarese Konkani is influenced by Marathi, the language of Maharashtra.

Tulsi Pooja

The picture above shows a traditional custom in Konkani culture called Tulsi pooja. It is traditionally carried out by married women in the month of Kartik Maas. During this time, the Tulsi plant, representing the goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity, is beautifully decorated with ornaments, colorful flowers and chalk. The plant is worshipped and an oil lamp is lit near it every evening. It is to celebrate the matrimonial binding of the Tulsi plant and Lord Vishnu, the omnipresent being and bring on prosperity.

Choodi Pooja

Choodi Pooja marks the beginning of the Shraavan month and is celebrated by married women.

Konkan Coastline

The Konkan coast is known for its beautiful beaches, lush greenery and abundant wildlife. On average, over 1 million international tourists visit just the state of Goa in a single year. The Konkan coast is east of the Arabian Sea and is west of the Deccan Plateau. Since there is a coastline, there is an abundance of fish which has clearly impacted Konkani culture since most dishes have seafood. Goa mostly consists of multiple islands with beaches on every coast.
